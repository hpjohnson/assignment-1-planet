Introduction to Mental Health

Mental health encompasses our emotional, social, and psychological wellbeing. Your mental state therefore greatly impacts how you percieve, and respond to everyday stressors. Good mental health allows you to effectively respond to any negative events, and deal with any results in a healthy manner. Bad mental health can lead to unhealthy coping mechanisms, which in turn could lead to a further decline in mental state. Constant periods of bad mental health, for example a prolonged period of stress, are well known to have negative effects on your physical health. It is therefore important to understand the signs of bad mental health, so they may be improved upon.

Mental Health Information

Symptoms in You

  • High or constant stress
  • Feelings of anxiety or fear
  • Depression, generally low mood
  • Unwarrented anger or hostility
  • Holing yourself away from friends and family
  • Visual or auditory hallucinations

Symptoms in Others

Mental health is a tricky topic to navigate for many people, so they may keep it to themselves. Being able to notice issues others are facing, and bringing the topic up can be a great way to encourage them to get the help they need. Large changes in mood, or general behaviour are generally the easiest to spot.

Helping Yourself

Seeing a specialist is not the only way to improve your own mental health. Activities that improve physical health can be of use - healthy eating, ensuring good sleep, physical exercise, and going outside can work wonders. Do a hobby for your own enjoyment to keep your mind busy. Speaking to family, friends, or other community about any issues is a great first step to improvment. A problem shared is a problem halved.

Words of Encouragement
